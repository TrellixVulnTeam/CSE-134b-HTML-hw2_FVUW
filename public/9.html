I've read articles where you've said cats think of us as big, stupid cats. Is that accurate? No. In the book [I say] that cats behave toward us in a way that's indistinguishable from [how] they would act toward other cats. They do think we're clumsy: Not many cats trip over people, but we trip over cats. But I don't think they think of us as being dumb and stupid, since cats don't rub on another cat that's inferior to them.
